About Me

Hi, I'm Dominic Yamarone. I'm from Los Angeles, CA and graduated from Northeastern University in Boston, MA. I majored in mechanical engineering with a minor in electrical engineering. My main interests are robotics and aerospace, especially in places where the two intersect. I also have a passion for music and worked as a DJ and writer for WRBB, the campus radio station. Here I got the chance to write album reviews and conduct interviews with some really talented musicians.

I did four internships during my college career. My first was at NASA JPL in La Cañada, CA. There I worked on cable harness engineering for the ECOSTRESS mission.

I also worked at Hasbro as part of the Integrated Play team. We focused on developing new forms of play with emerging technologies like VR, voice recognition, and computer vision. A few of the games I helped develop for Amazon Alexa became released by Amazon as their first games to work with Echo Buttons.

My second co op was at Endeavor Robotics, now known as FLIR Unmanned Ground Systems, a military robotics company that developed UGV systems for EOD and Search and Rescue. As a Systems Engineering, Instrumentation, and Test intern I developed and executed tests to verify that the systems met customer requirements. I also helped work on proposals that Endeavor has since been successfully awarded.

My third and final co op was at MORSE Corp in Cambridge, MA. MORSE is a fairly young defense startup that specializes in mission planning and airdrop. I worked on developing flight systems for the DARPA ICARUS program and other similar programs. My work allowed the team to test new designs more reliably and safely. I also helped build these flight systems and prepare them for test.

I recently finished my degree at Northeastern and graduated in December 2019. For my capstone design project I worked with a team to develop a robotic arm and haptic exoskeleton for the Avatar X-PRIZE challenge. I recently started full time at a robotics startup in Los Angeles. I'm working here as a Mechanical Engineer with responsibilities that range from early R&D to manufacturing of our systems. I feel that what I've learned in personal projects, co ops, and of course classes has helped prepare me to be confident in any career. As I continue to work on projects you can find summaries here. Thanks for checking out my website!
